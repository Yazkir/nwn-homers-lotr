Fire agate forms in thin brown layers that refract the incoming light, giving the gem its "fire." The refraction often forms patterns reminiscent of a dragon's scales, which makes it prized by sorcerers who claim to trace their lineage back to dragonkin. A well-cut stone has its outer layers polished and ground away until only the fire is visible.
