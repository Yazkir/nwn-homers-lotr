Tharbad.
entry #20 ¶
Tharbad is a port city that is well past its hay day. It has fallen under the influence of corrupt politicians and yet remains neutral to all to better its trade. There is work to be had here though. I would recommend the temple of East Tharbad. And the Tower of Alamar in West Tharbad. And for those willing to risk their neck, try the docks. There you can pay a fee to be ferry'd across to the Kallrist Islands.

Rivendell.
entry #19 ¶
The House of Elrond is a haven for those of Good. Many have sought refuge in the comfort of Rivendell. If you are to make a stop here, I would pay a visit to the smithy of Rivendell where you might find unique treasures. Or perhaps the Arrow Smith located in the Valley of Rivendell. If you are lucky enough to catch the Master at Arms, Glorfindel, talk to him a moment... it may be worth your while.

Carn Dum.
entry #18 ¶
The Evil fortress of Carn Dum is a staging point for those who follow Sauron. A dark place where the Black Numenoreans practice their dark rituals. It is advised that if you are a servant of Sauron you should venture through here once in a while. You might learn something of the cause you fight for. But be wary, There is very little here for the novice.

reply #31 ¶
Isengard.
entry #17 ¶
The tower of Isengard is a fearful place. Once beautiful gardens, now it has been turned into a warzone by the corrupt wizard Saruman. He has crossed orcs with goblins and is breeding an army to crush the Free Peoples. A fair place for those of moderate level to start out, if you are of evil incline. I would start here and venture to Dunland and the Dunland war party area. Or if you are brave/fool enough... Journey on to Moria.

Moria.
entry #16 ¶
Moria is the ancient city of the Dwarves; long has it been empty of their creators. The dwarves delved too greedily and too deep... and they awoke something in the dark place of the world that would have been best left sleeping. Now this foul resident resides there in, waiting to be uncovered. The gate of Moria still has the blessing of the Elves that constructed the gate. So for those who seek entrance, I have one word of advice to give you. 'Mellon'.

However the Dunland Areas still remain for low to moderate levels.
