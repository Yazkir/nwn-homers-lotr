1. SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Players/builders noticed that putting BOTH a 'plus enchant' (Enhancement)
bonus AND a 'plus attack' bonus on the same weapon (or on gauntlets worn with
a weapon) does not add up the way the numbers suggest. This is NOT a module
defect -- it is documented Neverwinter Nights engine behaviour. In NWN an
Enhancement Bonus is internally an attack bonus + a damage bonus bundled
together. A separate Attack Bonus item property does NOT stack with the attack
portion of an enhancement bonus: the engine uses the HIGHER of the two, not
the sum. So on every affected item the smaller of {enhancement, attack-bonus}
is silently wasted on the to-hit roll.

  (1) Single-item conflict (weapon has BOTH enh + attack): 253 blueprints
        - Player-obtainable weapons .......... 108
        - NPC-carried / store / unreachable .. 116
        - Monster natural weapons (claw/bite). 29
  (2) Cross-slot gauntlet conflict (gloves w/ attack bonus): 50 gloves
        These have NO on-item enhancement, but their attack bonus does NOT
        stack with the to-hit of any enhancement weapon the wearer wields.

2. THE RULE (why it happens)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  * Enhancement Bonus (item prop #6)  = attack bonus + damage bonus, bundled.
  * Attack Bonus (item prop #56)      = pure to-hit.
  * Enhancement attack portion and a separate Attack Bonus DO NOT STACK.
    The engine takes max(E, A) for to-hit -- never E + A.
    (Community test: a +1 enchant / +1 attack weapon hits at +1, not +2.)
  * This applies REGARDLESS OF SLOT: an attack bonus on GAUNTLETS also fails
    to stack with the weapon's enhancement bonus. Worn separately makes no
    difference.
  * DAMAGE bonuses DO stack with enhancement bonuses (different mechanic).
  * Enhancement still does work the attack bonus can't: it adds DAMAGE and
    helps bypass damage reduction. So enhancement is rarely the part to drop.
  * Attack Bonus vs. Alignment Group (#57) / vs. Racial Group (#58) ARE
    conditional and DO stack vs matching targets -- NOT buggy, left untouched.

  Sources:
   - https://nwn.fandom.com/wiki/Enhancement_bonus
   - https://nwn.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_bonus_(item_property)
   - https://steamcommunity.com/app/704450/discussions/0/689742575370785900/

3. REMEDIATION OPTIONS (shown per item as A / B below)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Option A -- REMOVE the redundant Attack Bonus property. When the attack bonus
is <= the enhancement bonus it is doing literally nothing, so deleting it
changes ZERO actual player power; it just cleans the property list and lowers
the item's listed gold cost.

Option B -- CONVERT the wasted attack bonus into a Damage Bonus of equal
magnitude. Damage bonuses DO stack with enhancement, so this preserves (or
raises) the item's power and makes the item as strong as its property list
implies. Pick a damage type (Magical is the safe default; or thematic).

  Property struct to edit (entry in PropertiesList.value[]):
     PropertyName=56 (Attack Bonus)  CostTable=2  CostValue=N  Subtype=0
  Option A: delete that struct.   Option B: change PropertyName 56 -> the
  Damage Bonus property id with the appropriate CostTable/CostValue.

5. GAUNTLETS / GLOVES -- CROSS-SLOT CONFLICT  (the 'gauntlet' half of the bug)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  No glove/gauntlet in the module carries an enhancement bonus, so none has an
  on-item conflict. The conflict is CROSS-SLOT: each glove below grants an
  Attack Bonus, and that to-hit does NOT add to the enhancement bonus of
  whatever weapon the character wields -- the engine uses the higher of the two.
  Effect: a wielder of, say, an enhancement +15 weapon gets ZERO extra to-hit
  from a +15 (or lower) attack-bonus gauntlet; only the excess above the
  weapon's enhancement does anything. For unarmed/monk builds the glove IS the
  weapon, so its attack bonus is the weapon's to-hit (still won't stack with
  Magic Weapon / enhancement effects).

  Fix per glove:
   A) Leave as-is if it is meant for high-attack unarmed builds (it works as
      the unarmed weapon's to-hit), OR
   B) Convert the glove's Attack Bonus -> a Damage Bonus (stacks with any
      weapon's enhancement) so the gauntlet always contributes regardless of
      what the wearer wields. Recommended for gauntlets meant to be worn
      alongside a melee weapon.
